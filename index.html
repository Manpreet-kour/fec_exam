Presentation techniques:

Planning is required. After planning is done, start outlining and structuring the presentation.

Choosing the mode of delivery is crucial to effectively convey the message.

Visual aids can enhance understanding. Use pie charts or graphs to depict the topic to the audience.

Guidelines for effective delivery include involving the audience by asking questions, utilizing visual aids, and maintaining a professional demeanor.

Body language and voice are important. Be polite, use appropriate gestures, maintain good posture, and dress formally.

Example on application of 7C's:

  • Clearness: Sarah ensures that her message about the change in deadlines is clear and easy to understand. She provides specific details about the new deadlines, including dates and any revised expectations.
  • Conciseness: Sarah keeps her message concise and to the point. Instead of overwhelming her team members with unnecessary information, she focuses on the key details of the change in deadlines.
  • Concreteness: Sarah provides concrete examples or reasons for the change in deadlines, such as unexpected delays in a supplier's delivery or additional client requirements. This helps her team members understand the rationale behind the decision.
  • Correctness: Sarah ensures that her message is factually accurate and free of errors. She double-checks the dates and details before communicating them to her team to avoid any misunderstandings.
  • Coherence: Sarah's message is coherent and well-organized. She presents the information in a logical sequence, starting with the reason for the change in deadlines and followed by the new deadlines and any related instructions or actions required from her team members.
  • Courtesy: Sarah communicates the change in deadlines with empathy and consideration for her team members' concerns. She acknowledges the impact that the change may have on their workload and expresses gratitude for their flexibility and cooperation.
  • Completeness: Sarah ensures that her message provides all the necessary information her team members need to understand the change in deadlines and take appropriate action. She encourages her team members to reach out if they have any questions or need further clarification.
